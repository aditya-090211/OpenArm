# Prototype History

Images in this folder document the evolution of OpenArm from its earliest versions.

---

## 2023 — Candy Stick Arm

The first version was built in 7th grade from candy sticks, micro servos (~₹150 each), cardboard, and an Arduino Uno. Two-joint arm, barely held its own weight, no structural integrity to speak of.

What it was good for: figuring out that servo control is harder than it looks, understanding mechanical linkages directly, and learning that geometry matters even at tiny scales.

File: `IMG20231125094533.jpg`

---

## 2024 — PVC Prototype

PVC pipe and hot glue. Higher-torque servos. Actually held a pose without drooping. Better range of motion, but still quite flexible.

This version was where I first really understood that structural rigidity isn't just about strength — flex makes the arm unpredictable in a way that servo control alone can't fix. Even with accurate commands, the end effector position was uncertain because the structure itself was moving under load.

Files: `Assembly 2.png`, `Final.png`, `Final (1).png`

---

## 2025 onward — CAD-Based Development

Moved to Onshape for all subsequent design work. Current renders are in `Photos/current/`. CAD iterations from 2025 onward are tracked in the Onshape document history rather than as separate image files here.
